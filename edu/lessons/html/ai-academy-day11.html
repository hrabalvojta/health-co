KAF Lens — How Kyndryl Thinks About This

How does Kyndryl structure every AI solution? This project must be designed as an agentic workflow, not a chatbot. In Kyndryl's Agentic AI Framework (KAF), every solution maps to these components:

🩹
Hospital Triage Nurse
Assesses patient, checks vitals, routes to the right specialist. Follows strict protocols. Escalates emergencies.
is like
🤖
Agent Router (Agentic Core)
Classifies ticket, checks policy, routes to the right handler. Follows guardrails. Escalates to human when needed.
⚙
Agentic Core
Orchestration, step planning, and tool-use — not just Q&A. The agent decides what to do, in what order, and which tools to call.
📦
Agentic Ingestion
Knowledge sources, business rules, processes, and integrations are "ingested" into the solution — not hardcoded.
📜
Policy-as-Code
Rules and compliance move from documents into an enforceable layer — guardrails by design, not afterthought.
🛡
Digital Trust / Run Safe
Audit logs, monitoring, validation, and safe production operations. Every action the agent takes must be traceable.
✋
Human-in-the-Loop
Clear decision points where a human takes control. Not "optional" — mandatory for high-risk and regulated workflows.
🔗
Interoperability (MCP / A2A)
Agents connect to tools (MCP) and to each other (A2A). Identity, authentication, and authorization at every boundary.
💡 KAF-native thinking: Every discovery question you write should connect to at least one KAF component. A good discovery packet shows you think in frameworks, not just features.

KAF distinguishes three governance tiers for agents:

  • Personal agents — used by a single user, low risk, minimal approval needed.
  • Team agents — shared within a department, moderate risk, team lead approval + registry entry.
  • Enterprise agents — customer-facing or cross-department, high risk, full governance approval, audit trail, monitoring.

EuroHealth's helpdesk agent is clearly an enterprise agent. That means full governance, registry, approval workflow, and production monitoring from Day 1. Your discovery must account for this.

Real-World Scenario
EuroHealth Insurance — The Situation
Context

EuroHealth Insurance AG, a mid-size insurer based in Frankfurt, operates across 8 EU countries with 3,000 employees. Their IT helpdesk used to handle 15,000 tickets/month with 12 full-time agents and a CSAT score of 3.2/5 — well below industry benchmark.

Recent Transformation (2025)

In late 2025, EuroHealth rolled out ServiceNow’s AI capabilities including Moveworks (acquired by ServiceNow, Dec 2025). The automated monitoring and AI-assisted ticket resolution reduced L1 ticket volume by ~35% and improved routing accuracy. Basic password resets and simple config questions are now handled autonomously. Headcount stayed at 12 FTE (works council agreement), but staff shifted to managing AI tools and handling escalations.

But the transformation created new problems: fragmented monitoring across ServiceNow, legacy tools, and departmental AI experiments. No unified governance. No audit trail for AI decisions. Shadow AI spreading — HR built their own chatbot, Claims has an unapproved LangChain prototype. CISO Stefan Weber flagged it to the board. The board wants an EU AI Act compliance framework by Q3 2026.

Why They’re Calling Kyndryl

The CIO, Hans Muller, doesn’t need another AI tool — he has tools. He needs someone who can industrialize what they already have: unify the fragmented AI landscape, build enforceable governance, create a scalable agentic platform that adapts to regulatory changes in days (not months), and make the whole thing auditable and production-safe. On-premises, EU AI Act governance-ready, operational within 6 months, €180K budget.

Your Mission

— Sprint 2 (built-in): Before continuing your own work, read 3 peer questions and react to at least one. Use 👍 (good question) or 🤔 (CIO wouldn’t care) or a short comment. Then continue your remaining 5 questions. Post any blockers: “🔴 I don’t know how to ask about X...”
