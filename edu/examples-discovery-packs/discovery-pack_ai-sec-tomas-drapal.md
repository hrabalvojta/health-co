## Client: EuroHealth Insurance AG

1️⃣ “Where does your current AI setup start to feel limited or out of control?”

Why it matters
Identifies governance blind spots and scaling bottlenecks beyond ServiceNow native controls.

Possible issue
Overreliance on vendor platform governance without independent oversight.

KAF Component: Core / Run Safe

Proposed KPIs

% of AI systems covered by centralized governance framework

% of AI workflows with full lifecycle documentation

Number of AI initiatives blocked due to governance gaps

2️⃣ “If regulators asked tomorrow how your AI systems work and how you control them, how quickly could you show proof?”

Why it matters
Measures audit readiness for EU AI Act compliance.

Possible issue
Manual evidence gathering, fragmented documentation.

KAF Component: Run Safe / Core

Proposed KPIs

Time to produce complete AI system audit package (target: <48h)

% of AI systems with documented risk classification

% of AI decisions traceable end-to-end

3️⃣ “Which AI rules exist only on paper, but are not automatically enforced by your systems?”

Why it matters
Distinguishes policy documentation from enforceable controls (Policy-as-Code).

Possible issue
Reliance on human discipline instead of technical guardrails.

KAF Component: Policy-as-Code

Proposed KPIs

% of AI policies translated into enforceable technical controls

% of AI requests automatically validated against compliance rules

Number of policy violations detected automatically per month

4️⃣ “When a new regulation or internal rule appears, how long does it take before your AI systems actually follow it?”

Why it matters
Measures regulatory agility and change management maturity.

Possible issue
Months-long adaptation cycles across departments.

KAF Component: Core / Policy-as-Code / Run Safe

Proposed KPIs

Regulatory change implementation lead time (target: weeks, not months)

% of AI systems updated within defined SLA after policy change

Number of systems operating under outdated rule sets

5️⃣ “Are there AI use cases you avoid because of compliance, security, or data concerns?”

Why it matters
Reveals structural blockers under on-prem and no-PII constraints.

Possible issue
Innovation frozen due to unclear guardrails.

KAF Component: Ingestion / Policy-as-Code

Proposed KPIs

Number of AI use cases approved after governance framework implementation

% of AI use cases classified by risk tier

Time from use-case proposal to compliance approval

6️⃣ “Do you have one clear overview of every AI system, model, or agent running across the company?”

Why it matters
EU AI Act requires inventory and risk classification.

Possible issue
Shadow AI and departmental silos.

KAF Component: Core

Proposed KPIs

% of AI systems registered in central inventory

% of AI systems assigned risk category (EU AI Act alignment)

Number of unregistered AI tools detected

7️⃣ “Where must a human always double-check or approve what AI decides?”

Why it matters
Ensures enforceable Human-in-the-Loop in high-impact insurance decisions.

Possible issue
Human validation informal or inconsistently applied.

KAF Component: HITL / Policy-as-Code

Proposed KPIs

% of high-risk AI decisions requiring enforced human approval

% of decisions logged with human validation record

Average time between AI recommendation and human confirmation

8️⃣ “If an AI system made a mistake or was manipulated, how would you detect it and stop it quickly?”

Why it matters
Tests AI-specific monitoring, containment, and incident response.

Possible issue
No AI-specific detection or containment playbooks.

KAF Component: Run Safe

Proposed KPIs

Mean Time to Detect (MTTD) AI-related incidents

Mean Time to Contain (MTTC) AI-related incidents

% of AI workflows covered by real-time monitoring

9️⃣ “What other systems will future AI agents need to connect to — and how tightly are those connections controlled?”

Why it matters
Agentic AI increases integration and privilege risk.

Possible issue
Shared accounts or excessive system permissions.

KAF Component: Interop / Run Safe

Proposed KPIs

% of AI integrations using least-privilege access

% of agent actions fully traceable to individual identity

Number of integrations using shared credentials

🔟 “At board level, is AI primarily seen as an efficiency tool — or as a regulated risk area?”

Why it matters
Determines strategic alignment and governance maturity.

Possible issue
AI not recognized as enterprise risk category.

KAF Component: Core

Proposed KPIs

Board-level AI risk reporting frequency

Existence of formal AI risk appetite statement (Yes/No → target: Yes)

% of AI initiatives reviewed under formal risk framework
