EuroHealth AI Discovery — One‑Page Executive Compression (AI‑PM)
Client: EuroHealth Insurance AG
Executive Sponsor: Hans Muller, CIO
Timeframe: 6 months
Budget: €180K
Board Success Criteria: 30% helpdesk cost reduction, AI unification, EU AI Act readiness by August 2026

1.Stakeholder Analysis (Executive View)

Hans Muller (CIO) is accountable for delivering a 30% reduction in EuroHealth’s remaining helpdesk costs, unifying AI currently split across ServiceNow, HR, and Claims, and demonstrating EU AI Act compliance to the board within six months, with personal executive credibility at stake.
EuroHealth Board evaluates success based on measurable cost reduction, elimination of shadow AI, and regulatory readiness ahead of the August 2026 EU AI Act deadline, requiring clear metrics and governance rather than technical depth.
Helpdesk Team (Jan + 12 agents) operates ServiceNow with Moveworks and explicitly fears replacement, making adoption behavior a direct dependency for realizing cost savings.
Legal, Security, and Compliance hold effective veto power due to the non‑negotiable on‑prem requirement and EU AI Act deadline, with ownership and alignment currently undefined.
HR and Claims AI Owners run independent AI solutions (HR chatbot, Claims LangChain prototype) without governance and are directly impacted by the requirement to bring all AI under one platform.

2.Key Findings and Implications

EuroHealth’s core issue is AI sprawl without governance, not lack of AI capability.
The 30% cost reduction is a board KPI, not a delivery scope, requiring explicit baselines and reporting.
EU AI Act compliance by August 2026 is a defined success condition, not a background constraint.
The on‑prem‑only mandate materially increases delivery risk and reduces mitigation options.
A 30% outdated, ownerless knowledge base limits achievable automation.
Helpdesk fear of replacement makes change management a delivery dependency, not an optional activity.

3.Technical / Functional Focus Areas

Define what “AI under one platform” means for EuroHealth (ServiceNow/Moveworks, HR chatbot, Claims prototype).
Establish EuroHealth‑wide governance for auditability, traceability, and policy enforcement.
Assign EU AI Act classification ownership and define compliance evidence for board reporting.
Validate on‑prem compute and GPU feasibility within the €180K / six‑month constraint.
Assign ownership and remediation for the EuroHealth knowledge base.
Define helpdesk cost and automation baselines and measurement logic.
Identify EuroHealth‑specific change‑management actions for helpdesk adoption.

4.Key Risks (Board‑Relevant)

On‑prem feasibility risk: GPU and compute costs unconfirmed within €180K.
Compliance ownership risk: EU AI Act responsibility across Legal, Security, and IT undefined.
Knowledge quality risk: Outdated content undermines automation accuracy.
Adoption risk: Helpdesk resistance may suppress realized savings.
Scope‑to‑budget risk: AI unification across ServiceNow, HR, and Claims may exceed budget.
Measurement risk: Undefined baselines could misalign board expectations.

5.Immediate Next Steps (First 2 Weeks)

Confirm program framing as AI unification and governance, not a single use case.
Validate on‑prem compute and GPU feasibility against the €180K constraint.
Assign EU AI Act ownership and define board‑level compliance evidence.
