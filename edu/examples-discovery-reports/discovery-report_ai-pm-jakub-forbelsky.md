# DISCOVERY REPORT -- AI-PM

Client: EuroHealth Insurance AG\
Sponsor: Hans Muller (CIO)\
Role: AI Product Manager (Kyndryl AI Consulting Team)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Executive Summary

EuroHealth has initiated multiple AI automation efforts across
departments (IT, HR, Claims). While early automation exists, these
initiatives operate without unified governance, compliance framework, or
centralized platform strategy.

To achieve the stated objective of 30% helpdesk cost reduction and EU AI
Act compliance readiness by August 2026, a structured industrialization
approach is required. Cost reduction is a measurable outcome; governance
and consolidation are the foundational scope.

The recommended approach is phased delivery under an L2 (Constrained
Autonomy) model, beginning with L1 auto-resolution within IT helpdesk.
Early validation of infrastructure, KPI baselines, and regulatory
alignment is required to ensure feasibility and maintain executive-level
confidence.

## 2. Autonomy Level Declaration

Design target: L2 -- Constrained Autonomy

-   AI auto-resolves defined L1 tickets\
-   Human supervision and override required\
-   L3 autonomy excluded due to regulatory and operational risk

This autonomy level balances measurable cost reduction with compliance
and risk control.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Key Findings & Implications

### 3.1 Scope Clarification

Cost reduction is a board-level metric. The underlying requirement is AI
governance consolidation and platform unification.

### 3.2 Executive Visibility

Structured executive reporting and milestone validation are critical
success factors.

### 3.3 Constraint Validation Required

The following constraints require validation in Week 1: - On-prem
infrastructure capacity\
- Budget allocation (hardware inclusion)\
- Current automation baseline\
- Knowledge base quality\
- Compliance classification

### 3.4 Timeline Feasibility

The 6-month target requires phased delivery and controlled scope for
Phase 1.

### 3.5 Expansion Potential

Successful IT deployment enables structured rollout to Claims and other
departments.
