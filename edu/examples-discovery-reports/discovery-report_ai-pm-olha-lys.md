# 3. Core Findings That Change the Approach

## 3.1 Compliance Hierarchy

If trade-offs arise, compliance readiness takes precedence over cost reduction.

Decision order:
Compliance → Governance → ROI → Expansion.

---

## 3.2 Escalation Leakage Is the Real Economic Lever

Ticket volume is stable.  
Cost growth stems from:

- L2 escalation  
- Misrouting  
- Duplicate handling  
- Knowledge search inefficiency  

Given ~35% L1 automation already exists, incremental chatbot improvement alone cannot deliver 30%.

Escalation reduction must be the primary ROI driver.

### 3.2A Economic Feasibility Check (30% Stress Test)

The 30% target must be validated against:

- Remaining manual workload categories  
- Escalation rate contribution to total cost  
- Rework and duplicate handling costs  
- Cost per L2 ticket vs L1 resolution  

If escalation leakage does not account for a sufficient share of remaining operational cost, the 30% target must be recalibrated or supported by process changes beyond AI.

This validation is required in Week 1 before automation scope is finalized.

---

## 3.3 Governance Debt & AI Consolidation Mandate

Current state:

- Moveworks in IT  
- HR chatbot outside central governance  
- Claims prototype unregistered  
- No unified AI ownership registry  

Industrialization requires enforceable consolidation.

### Phase 1 Governance Mandate

- **Mandatory AI Registration:** Every AI system must be registered with a named accountable owner.
- **Onboard or Sunset Rule:** AI tools must either be integrated under governance controls or formally sunset via executive-approved exception process.
- **Time-Bound Exceptions:** All deviations logged and board-visible.
- **Executable Policy Enforcement:** Governance must operate via runtime controls (Policy-as-Code).

Governance authority must remain actively enforced to be credible.

If executive mandate weakens or departmental resistance is tolerated, shadow AI will persist and erode control claims. Industrialization success therefore depends not only on technical enforcement, but on sustained executive backing across HR and Claims.

---

## 3.4 Knowledge Base Risk Is Commercial

~30% outdated content introduces:

- Incorrect answers at scale  
- Escalation spikes  
- Trust erosion in early rollout  

Automation must focus on high-confidence categories first.  
Content ownership is a prerequisite for ROI.

---

## 3.5 Adoption Is a Financial KPI

Helpdesk team fears replacement.

If adoption fails:

- Escalation leakage persists  
- Automation metrics distort  
- ROI collapses  

Human-in-the-loop transparency and override clarity are commercial safeguards.

---

## 3.6 Change Management Strategy (Helpdesk Adoption)

Cost reduction depends on behavior change.

### Phase 1 Change Principles

1. **Co-Pilot Framing**
   - AI suggests; human approves in early rollout.
2. **Override Transparency**
   - One-click override; logged and visible.
3. **Role Evolution Clarity**
   - Shift from repetitive execution to supervised resolution.
4. **Measured Adoption**
   - Weekly tracking of usage rate and override patterns.

Adoption consistency is required for financial KPI realization.
